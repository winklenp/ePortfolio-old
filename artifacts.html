Definition

My definition of interdisciplinarity is combining more than one discipline in order to create a more well rounded citizen.

Metaphor

My metaphor for interdisciplinarity is of fire and gasoline. The fire is the initial passion. (for me its programming) The gasoline are the 'accessory' disiplines that feed the fire of the initial passion. By combining disciplines you reach deeper levels of understanding and effectivness than you would have otherwise.

Reflection

To be honest, I joined the interdisciplinary program because it was the fastest way to get my degree. I have been in school for 5 years now, have taken too many classes, and have already been working in my field. When my previous advisor recommended the degree to me - it sounded perfect, but I didnt know anything about it. After joining, however, I have found that I should have been in this degree from the beginning. It is exactly what I was looking for - learning about my interests and creating the degree plan that best works for me. It has allowed me to study topics like traditional computer science, as well as IT game development, and philosophy and communications courses. I have thouroughly enjoyed this degree program.

I believe that this style of study will spread like wildfire across the country - especially with the advent on online learning. Students are already leading their learning in online school, it makes sense for them to craft the degree themselves (within reason).
